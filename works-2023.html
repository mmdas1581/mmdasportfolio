Works 2023

  • Directional Diversity
  • Medium: Terracotta, Wood
  • Location: Sree Sankaracharya University of Sanskrit, Kalady

Concept note: Directional Diversity is a sculptural exploration of the fragmented relationship between human identity and the natural environment. Crafted from raw terracotta and mounted on wood, the work utilises fundamental, earth-based materials to reflect on our intrinsic physical connection to the land. The diverse animal forms represent the varied ecologies and species disrupted by human expansion. Placed on individual pedestals and facing different directions, they symbolise the disorientation and conflicting paths we navigate in our modern relationship with nature. It is a personal reflection on witnessing ecological shifts, capturing the silent, expressive tension of the natural world.
