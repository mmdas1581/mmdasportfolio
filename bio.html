Artist Biography

Manu M Das (b. 2001, Kerala) is an installation artist and spatial practitioner operating at the intersection of rural ecological memory and metropolitan extraction. Rooted in a personal history of various manual labor including working as a postman, Das brings a visceral understanding of physical endurance and structural exhaustion to his practice. Splitting his time between Palakkad and New Delhi, he transforms the mechanics of urban displacement and architectural hostility into precarious, experimental environments.

By utilizing discarded institutional wreckage, raw concrete, shattered glass, industrial grease, and construction rebar, and by forcing these industrial materials into violent collisions with bio-receptive elements and the living body, his installations and lens-based documentation capture the exact friction of surviving within the urban margins.

Das is currently pursuing an MFA in Sculpture at Jamia Millia Islamia, researching material entropy and the ecological casualties of rapid urbanization.
