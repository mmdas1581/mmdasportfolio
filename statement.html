Artist Statement

My art is a physical interrogation of the urban transaction, driven by my history working within commercial supply chains as a delivery agent, postman, and labourer. Having navigated the city’s hidden logistical pathways, I view urban commerce as an inescapable, predatory machine.

My work centres on what I term "Transactional Archetypes"-mass-produced, foundational items of consumption like unglazed kulhad cups, standardised textiles, and soft drink cans. I do not treat these items as generic waste; I frame them as repositories of Material Memory that perfectly symbolise a complete, rapid cycle of human use and erasure.

My methodology contrasts rigorous analytical mapping with visceral physical violence. I log the life story and GPS coordinates of these archetypes, and then force them against non-conventional industrial remnants-corroded gears, barbed wire, and heavy machinery parts. In my installations, these industrial elements function as predatory extraction mechanisms designed to physically erode, jam, and instantly nullify the memory of the softer artefacts. By manipulating and destroying these objects, I visualise the precise point where mass-culture artefacts transition into permanent refuse, physically mapping Delhi NCR’s progression from raw soil to a manufactured geology of disposal.
