Artist Statement

Informed by a personal history within commercial and logistical chains—from a childhood in a family shop to roles as a construction laborer, postman, and delivery agent—my practice investigates urban transactions as the shadow of contemporary civilization. Framed by Political Ecology, I interrogate how material history hardens into a physical topography.

My methodology employs rigorous analytical data logging via GPS coordinates and physical labels, contrasted against visceral material compression. I work with "Transactional Archetypes"—mass-produced artifacts like unglazed kulhad cups and standardized bleached textiles—framed as repositories of "Material Memory."

I pit these soft archetypes against non-conventional industrial remnants like corroded gears and barbed wire, which function as predatory extraction mechanisms designed to physically erode and nullify that memory. My installations visualize the precise point where mass-culture artifacts become waste, analyzing Delhi NCR’s progression from raw soil to a new, manufactured geology of disposal.
