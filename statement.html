Artist Statement

I build physical environments that confront the reality of the rapidly expanding city. I construct immersive, precarious spaces that make ecological collapse and urban displacement undeniable. My work is built from the debris of the metropolis. I use the very materials that drive the city’s expansion - raw concrete, suffocating plaster dust, shattered street glass, and construction steel - and force them into direct conflict with natural, earth-based elements.

By dragging these industrial materials into the gallery and stripping them of their intended utility, I expose the heavy, destructive footprint they leave behind. The resulting installations are restrictive and hostile by design. They require the viewer to physically navigate the weight, dust, and sharp edges of the city's waste.

Through this physical friction, my practice asks a central question: what happens to organic life and marginalized communities when they are constantly paved over by the relentless infrastructure of the modern capital?
