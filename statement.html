Artist Statement

The history is inside the work.

The body that absorbs what systems discard has no record. I make that record visible. The history is not behind my practice. It is inside it. I work with quarried hillsides, construction corridors, plaster waste, discarded beds, advertisement pamphlets and dust because they hold evidence.

The work asks what the city takes from land and from bodies, and what gets left behind. The encounter has to cost something. It has to leave a mark. Anything less is decoration.

My central concern is what I call the material memory of manufactured geologies: the new physical landscapes produced not by tectonic time but by capital time - quarry voids, textile waste accumulations, carbon corridors where forest ecosystems once processed light into life.
