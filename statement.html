Artist Statement

My art is driven by my bodily history working within commercial supply chains as a delivery agent, postman, and labourer in Palakkad, Kerala . My work is a physical interrogation of the urban transactions. I view urban commerce as an inescapable, predatory machine that is rapidly manufacturing a geology of disposal - an architecture of extraction I document across whichever ecology I currently occupy.

My work centres on ‘Material Memory of manufactured geologies’. Materials i use mostly are mass-produced, foundational items of consumption like unglazed kulhads cups, standardized textiles, industrial elements and institutional materials. I log their life cycles and GPS coordinates, framing them as repositories of Material Memory.

My methodology contrasts rigorous analytical mapping with visceral physical violence. I force these soft artifacts - and the very archives I create to document them - against non-conventional industrial remnants. In my practice, these industrial elements function as extraction mechanisms. Whether I am crushing a mass-culture artifact or pressing carbon residue into an experimental publication, I am visualizing the precise point of erasure.
